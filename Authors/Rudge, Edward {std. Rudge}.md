### Author

Rudge, Edward (1763-1846), British (English) botanist and antiquary, living on the Abbey estate at Evesham. 
**Abbreviated name**: *Rudge*

#### Herbarium and types

[[Collection BM|BM]]. – The Guyana plants colleced by Joseph Martin are in the Rudge herbarium. They were captured from the French in May 1803 and acquired by Rudge and Lambert. Circa 400 Martin specimens were bought by BM at the Lambert sale; another 772 were included in Rudge's herbarium. Stearn and Williams (1957) give a list of the plants described by Rudge with indication of types and synonymy. Rudge's own herbarium, consisting of 4318 specimens (in addition to the Martin specimens) was presented to [[Collection BM|BM]] in 1847. It contained e.g. Dickson's exsiccatae, Fuci from Dawson Turner, Ericae from R.A. Salisbury. Some further specimens from Rudge's herbarium (not collected by him) are at [[Collection FI|FI]].
