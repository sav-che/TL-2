### Author

Van Geel, Pierre Corneille (1796-1836), Belgian clergyman, botanist and polemical publicist; ordained 1815; with Drapiez founder of a "horticultural establishment" at Bruxelles \[later Jardin botanique de l'État, Jardin national de botanique de Belgique\]; later residing in The Hague and Paris. 
**Abbreviated name**: *==Name in the standard form is not specified==.*

Unknown.

### Publications

##### n.15.904. Sertum botanicum

**Title**
*Sertum botanicum*. Collection choisie de plantes remarquables par leur utilité, leur élégance, leur éclat ou leur nouveauté; consistant en six cents planches... accompagnées d'un texte particulier pour chaque plante (encyclographie du règne végétal)... par une société de botanistes et dirigée par P.C. van Geel. Bruxelles \[1827-\] 1828-1832 \[-1836\], 4 vols. Fol.
**Abbreviated title**: *Sert. bot.*

**Notes**
*Authorship*: Van Geel was certainly the editor and possibly the author of the *Sertum*. H. Coninckx (1938) suggests that Pierre Auguste Joseph Drapiez (1778-1856) may have been responsible for the text.
*Publ*.: 1827-1836 probably in 100 livraisons of 6 plates each. Fasc. 1 and 2 were published in Apr 1827 (Rev. Bibl. Pays Bas 6: 139). The six hundred (or 594?) plates are unnumbered hand-coloured lithographs by G. Severeyns, who also supplied the one uncoloured plate. T.T. Barnard (in lit. 28 Oct 1972) informs us that he consulted the copies at BM, K and LINN; in each copy the plates are in a different order (K: Linnaean system; BM alphabetic by genera, LINN taxonomically). The prospectus issued by the publisher (copies BR, G, K) states that the fascicles will consist of 6 plates and 3-4 sheets of text, 600 plates to be published in 3 years. We saw additional copies at BR and G. – A number of new taxa was described in this work and their dating poses a problem (see e.g. T.T. Barnard, J.S. Afr. Bot. suppl. 10: 46). – Many plates are probably pirated (Blunt, GF), the text, however, contains original elements.
*Reissue*: *Flore des Serres et Jardins de Paris*, Paris 1834; see NI 2255.
*Note*: The text of the *Encyclographie du règne végétal*, Bruxelles 1833-1838; NI 2241 is sometimes also attributed to Van Geel; the editor was probably Drapiez.
