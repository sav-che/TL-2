### Author

Shaw, George Russell (1848-1937), American architect and botanist; practicing architecture with Henry Hunnewell at Boston 1873-1902. 
**Abbreviated name**: *Shaw* \[standard form in IPNI: *Shaw*\]

#### Herbarium and types

[[Collection A|A]] (*Pinus* collection separate); further material at [[Collection K|K]], [[Collection MICH|MICH]], [[Collection NY|NY]], [[Collection P|P]]. – Manuscripts and letters at [[Collection GH|GH]].

#### Bibliography and biography

Barnhart 3: 266 (b. 28 Oct 1848, d. 15 Jan 1937); BJI 2: 163; BM 8: 1184; Hortus 3: 1204; Langman p. 697; MW p. 445; SIA 237; Tucker 1: 647.

#### Biofile

- Altamirano, F., Anal. Inst. Med. nac. Mex. 6(2): 341-355. 1904 (on a trip with G.R. Shaw, notes on Pinus), also in Bol. Soc. Mich. geogr. Est. 3: 223, 226, 242. 1907 (n.v., fide Langman).
- Anon., Boston Globe 16 Jan 1937 (typewritten copy NY).
- McVaugh, R., Contr. Univ. Mich. Herb. 9: 310. 1972 (coll. Mexico).
- Shaw, G.R., Gard. Chron. ser. 3: 35: 179-180. 1904, 36: 98. 6 Aug 1904 (The Times of Cuba; The Pines of Western Cuba; also reprinted).

#### Eponymy

*Shawia* J.R. Forster et J.G.A. Forster (1776) was named for Thomas Shaw (1693-1751), traveller in the Levant; *Shawiella* Hansford (1957) was named for D. Shaw, a plant collector in New Guinea.
